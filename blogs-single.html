From Consultation to Action: How Local Voices Helped Shape Madhya Pradesh’s Climate Strategy

John Adams Copy link

11 June 2025

Copy link Instagram LinkedIn X (Formerly Twitter)

Heading

Climate policy often begins on paper. But at ASAR, we believe it only becomes real when it reaches the ground. That belief shaped our partnership with the Madhya Pradesh State Climate Cell and Department of Environment in 2023–24.



The Challenge

Madhya Pradesh was in the process of updating its State Climate Action Strategy. But like many such plans, the risk was that it would be designed in isolation—without the voices of those who would live with its outcomes.

Our Approach

ASAR stepped in to support a different kind of process—one that was collaborative, inclusive, and rooted in the realities of communities.

  • We facilitated multi-stakeholder consultations across 6 districts.
  • Participants included CSOs, women’s groups, youth networks, tribal communities, and local governance representatives.
  • Discussions covered a range of climate issues: heat, water, livelihoods, energy, mobility—always viewed through a local lens
  • We also co-developed planning tools that supported this process, including:
  • Region-specific climate vulnerability mapping templates
  • Frameworks for integrating gender and social equity into local climate action
  • Capacity-building sessions for district planning teams

Voices That Shaped the Strategy

“ASAR helped turn an abstract plan into something people could see themselves in.”
— State Climate Officer, Dept. of Environment, Madhya Pradesh

What Changed

By the end of the engagement:

  • Local concerns were directly reflected in the revised strategy
  • Over 100 stakeholders had meaningfully contributed to the planning process
  • Government teams were better equipped to carry the work forward
  • Equity and inclusion were no longer add-ons—they were central to the vision

Conclusion

Our work in Madhya Pradesh reinforced a powerful truth: climate action becomes meaningful when it's co-created. By centering community voices, strengthening institutional capacity, and weaving equity into every layer of the process, we helped shape a strategy that reflects the people it’s meant to serve. This isn’t just a success story—it’s a model for how climate planning can be done differently: locally rooted, collaboratively built, and built to last.
